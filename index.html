About me

Engineer by training,
researcher by passion.

I am a research-driven Telecommunication Engineering graduate from the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), Ghana, with a strong foundation in wireless communications, machine learning, and radio wave propagation modelling.

My MPhil research focused on improving path loss models for 5G systems in the Sub-6 GHz spectrum, using the KNUST campus as a real-world testbed. I am particularly passionate about Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC), channel modelling, and AI-driven signal analysis — the frontier of beyond-5G/6G networks.

Beyond research, I have accumulated several years of teaching and laboratory mentorship experience, having guided students through Digital Signal Processing, MATLAB, and RF instrumentation at KNUST. I attended the World Radiocommunication Conference (WRC-23) in Dubai, connecting with the global wireless community.
